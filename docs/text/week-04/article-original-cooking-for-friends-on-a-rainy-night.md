# Cooking for Friends on a Rainy Night
> Source: Dayread Original | Date: March 2026
> Note: Week 4 — Short Personal Narratives (difficulty 2)

It started raining around five o'clock, and by seven the streets were empty and shining under the streetlights.

I had invited three friends over for dinner, but I had not decided what to cook until they were already on their way.

I opened the refrigerator and found some vegetables, a block of cheese, and half a bag of pasta.

That was enough to make something warm and simple, so I started boiling water and chopping onions.

The kitchen filled with steam, and the windows fogged up from the heat inside.

My friends arrived one by one, each one shaking rain off their jacket at the door.

We crowded into the small kitchen together, and someone turned on music while I stirred the sauce.

The pasta was not perfect — I added too much salt — but nobody seemed to mind.

We sat on the floor around a low table because I only had two chairs at the time.

After dinner, we talked for hours while the rain kept tapping against the window.

Looking back, that evening was one of the best I had all winter, not because the food was special but because everything felt easy and unplanned.

The best meals are not always the ones you prepare for days; sometimes they are the ones you make in thirty minutes with whatever you have.
