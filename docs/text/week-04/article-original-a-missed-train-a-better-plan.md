# A Missed Train, A Better Plan
> Source: Dayread Original | Date: March 2026
> Note: Week 4 — Short Personal Narratives (difficulty 2)

I was supposed to catch the 8:15 train to the city center last Friday.

I left my apartment at 7:50, thinking twenty-five minutes was more than enough.

But when I reached the station, the departure board showed that the train had already left three minutes early due to a schedule adjustment.

The next one was not until 9:00, so I sat down on a metal bench and stared at the tracks.

At first, I felt annoyed because I had planned every part of my morning carefully.

Then I noticed a small coffee shop across the street that I had never seen before.

I walked over, ordered a black coffee, and sat by the window while I waited.

The shop was warm and quiet, and the owner told me he had opened it just two weeks ago.

We talked about the neighborhood for a few minutes, and he recommended a bookstore nearby that I later visited on my way home.

When the 9:00 train finally arrived, I got on with a warm cup in my hand and a new place saved in my memory.

I realized the delay had given me something the original plan never included.

Sometimes the best discoveries happen when your schedule breaks, and you stop rushing long enough to look around.
