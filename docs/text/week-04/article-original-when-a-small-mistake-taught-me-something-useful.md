# When a Small Mistake Taught Me Something Useful
> Source: Dayread Original | Date: March 2026
> Note: Week 4 — Short Personal Narratives (difficulty 2)

A few months ago, I sent an email to the wrong person at work.

It was not a serious message — just a short note about a meeting time — but it went to a manager I had never spoken to before.

I noticed the mistake five seconds after I pressed send, and my face turned warm immediately.

I quickly wrote a follow-up email apologizing for the confusion.

To my surprise, the manager replied within a few minutes and said it was no problem at all.

She even added that the meeting topic sounded interesting and asked if she could join.

That accidental email led to a useful conversation and eventually to a small project we worked on together.

When I told a colleague about it later, he laughed and said most of his best contacts started from similar accidents.

After that experience, I stopped feeling so anxious about small mistakes in communication.

I started to see them as moments that could go in any direction, depending on how I responded.

Not every error leads to something good, of course, but many of them matter far less than we imagine in the first five seconds.

The important thing is what you do after the mistake, not the mistake itself.
