# The Day My Phone Died Too Early
> Source: Dayread Original | Date: March 2026
> Note: Week 4 — Short Personal Narratives (difficulty 2)

Last Tuesday, I left home in a hurry and forgot to charge my phone the night before.

By noon, the battery had already dropped to twelve percent, and I still had three meetings left.

I turned off every app I could think of and lowered the brightness until the screen was barely visible.

During the second meeting, my phone buzzed once and then went dark in the middle of a message I was reading.

Without my phone, I could not check the time, look up the address of the restaurant where I was meeting a friend, or even call a taxi.

I stood outside the office building for a moment, unsure what to do next.

Then I remembered that the restaurant was near the subway station I used to pass every day on my old commute.

I walked there from memory, arrived ten minutes late, and found my friend already eating bread at the table.

She laughed when I explained why I was late and said she had survived the same thing last month.

That evening, I plugged in my phone the moment I walked through the door.

The experience reminded me how much I depend on a small device that fits in my pocket.

It also reminded me that most problems feel bigger than they actually are once you start solving them.
