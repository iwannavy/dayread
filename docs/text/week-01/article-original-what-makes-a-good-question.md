# What Makes a Good Question

A good question is more than a sentence with a question mark.

It is a clear opening that invites thought.

Good questions guide attention, but they leave room for more than one answer.

They do not hide behind vague words or ask five things at once.

In class, at work, and in daily conversation, a strong question begins with real curiosity.

It tries to understand, not to impress.

"What changed after the new rule?" is easier to answer than "What do you think about everything?"

Good questions improve conversations because they create direction without closing the door.
