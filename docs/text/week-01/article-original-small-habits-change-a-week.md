# How Small Habits Change a Week

Big change rarely begins with a heroic morning.

It usually begins with one small action that returns each day.

A person puts a book on the table, writes one sentence after breakfast, or reviews five words before sleep.

These acts look too small to matter on Monday.

By Thursday, however, they start to shape the rhythm of the week.

When a task appears at the same time and place, the brain needs less energy to begin.

That is why a short habit often survives while a grand plan disappears.

Over one week, tiny repeats become visible progress, and visible progress makes the next repeat easier.
