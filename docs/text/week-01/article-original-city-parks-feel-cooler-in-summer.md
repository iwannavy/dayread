# Why City Parks Feel Cooler in Summer

On a hot afternoon, a city park often feels cooler than the street beside it.

Trees give shade, so sunlight does not strike the ground as directly.

Leaves also release water into the air, and that process helps cool the space nearby.

Grass and soil store less heat than concrete.

Dark roads and walls absorb sunlight quickly, so busy blocks stay warm longer.

Parks slow this pattern because they break up the hard surfaces that trap heat.

Wind moves more easily through open green space, and people feel that movement on their skin.

A small park cannot remove summer heat, but it can give the city a place to breathe.
